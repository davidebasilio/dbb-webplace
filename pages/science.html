Research

In a broad sense, the goal of my research is improving the efficiency of computation.

For my PhD, I focused on system-level mechanisms and policies across the architectural and operating system levels, to improve energy and cost efficiency of datacenters.

The two main projects of my dissertation are AutoPro, a goal-oriented resource provisioning scheme for public cloud computing (developed at the NECST Lab, within the CHANGE research group), and Rubik, a DVFS management system that maximizes the energy efficiency of batch applications while enforcing tail latency QoS of latency-sensitive applications (developed with Prof. Daniel Sanchez's group at MIT CSAIL).

Starting from February 2015, I will join Prof. Lothar Thiele's group at ETH as a postdoc researcher.
