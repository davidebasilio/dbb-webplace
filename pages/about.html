About

"I am neither especially clever nor especially gifted. I am only very, very curious." Albert Einstein

My name is Davide Basilio Bartolini and am currently a PhD student at the Dipartimento di Elettronica e Informazione of Politecnico di Milano. My advisor is Prof. Marco Domenico Santambrogio and my PhD tutor is Prof. Donatella Sciuto.

I was born in Brescia, Italy on July 5, 1987 and I spent my first years with my family first in Rino di Sonico, Valle Camonica, and then moved to Provezze, Franciacorta. My education went all the way up to getting a high school diploma from Liceo Scientifico Madonna della Neve in 2006 and then I enrolled to the bachelor degree in Computer Engineering at Politecnico di Milano. I achieved my B.Sc in 2009 and then enrolled in the joint master degree program in Computer Science and Engineering between Politecnico di Milano and University of Illinois at Chicago; I spent the Fall 2010 semester at Chicago, IL and achieved a M.Sc degree from UIC in May 2011 and from Politecnico in December 2011. My theses are publicly available in the publications section of this website. Right after graduating, in January 2012 I began my PhD at Politecnico and I am currently a third-year PhD student at the Dipartimento di Elettronica e Informazione. In February 2015, I will join Prof. Lothar Thiele's group at ETH as a postdoc researcher.

I am interested in computing systems research and I mainly deal with operating systems, autonomic computing, evolvable hardware, and embedded systems. More information about my research can be found in the science section. The teaching section contains material for courses that I taught at Politecnico di Milano during my PhD.

I am affiliated as a student member to the Association for Computing Machinery (ACM) and to the European Network of Excellence on High Performance and Embedded Architecture and Compilation (HiPEAC).

Apart from research, I practice rock climbing and mountaneering and I am a rock climbing instructor at CAI SEM in Milano, Italy. I am also interested in photography and music (I used to play piano and guitar, but I am not practicing very much). The images section contains a few pictures I took, mainly from my climbing trips. I used to enjoy writing fiction, but I haven't had much time for it in recent years; I'm trying to get back to it, I might post something in the words section.
